# Genre: Folk / Acoustic

## Characteristics
Folk is rooted in acoustic instruments — guitar, banjo, fiddle. Very high acousticness (0.8–0.98). Low-to-moderate energy (0.2–0.5). Tempo 65–100 BPM.

## Mood associations
- **Relaxed**: Warm, unhurried, storytelling.
- **Melancholy**: Minor-key folk, emotionally heavy.
- **Happy**: Upbeat folk, campfire singalong energy.

## Listener context
Quiet evenings, nature, coffee shops, introspective moments. Folk listeners usually want music that feels human and unpolished.

## Related genres
Country, classical, acoustic singer-songwriter. Folk listeners often also enjoy jazz or lofi.

## Recommendation tips
Acousticness is the single most important signal for folk fans. A song with acousticness > 0.85 will feel "right" even if the genre label doesn't match exactly.
