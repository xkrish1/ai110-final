# Genre: EDM (Electronic Dance Music)

## Characteristics
EDM is built for dancefloors — massive drops, synthesized bass, and relentless energy. Energy is very high (0.85–0.98). Tempo ranges from 128–145 BPM for house, up to 170+ for drum & bass.

## Mood associations
- **Intense**: The default EDM mood — overwhelming, euphoric energy.
- **Happy**: Festival EDM — bright synths, uplifting melodies, crowd-friendly drops.
- **Energetic**: Pre-workout and hype scenarios — maximum BPM, aggressive bass.

## Listener context
Clubs, festivals, gym sessions, gaming, running. EDM demands active attention — it is designed to be felt physically, not just heard.

## Related genres
Synthwave, hip-hop (for high-energy overlap), pop (dance-pop crossover). EDM fans wanting variety often enjoy synthwave or high-energy pop.

## Recommendation tips
Energy similarity is the dominant signal for EDM fans — anything below 0.80 will feel like a let-down. Danceability above 0.85 is a strong positive signal. Acousticness should be near zero.
