# Genre: Pop

## Characteristics
Pop music is built around catchy melodies, verse-chorus structures, and broad emotional appeal. Energy levels typically range from moderate (0.6) to very high (0.95). Tempo usually falls between 100–140 BPM.

## Mood associations
- **Happy / Upbeat**: The most common pop mood. High valence (0.75+), danceable, radio-friendly.
- **Intense**: High-energy pop (think workout anthems) — energy above 0.85, fast tempo.
- **Melancholy pop**: Lower valence but still polished production.

## Listener context
Pop works well for commutes, workouts, social gatherings, and background listening. It rarely demands full attention, making it versatile.

## Related genres
Indie pop, synth-pop, dance-pop, electropop. If someone enjoys pop they may also enjoy synthwave or indie pop.

## Recommendation tips
For happy pop fans: prioritize genre + mood match. For high-energy pop: weight energy similarity heavily. Valence above 0.75 is a strong positive signal.
